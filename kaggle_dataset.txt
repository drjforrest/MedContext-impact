# MedContext Three-Dimensional Validation Dataset

## Overview

A purpose-built validation dataset of 160 medical image-claim pairs designed to evaluate
contextual authenticity detection — the ability to assess whether a medical image's
accompanying textual claim is both medically accurate and supported by the visual evidence.

This dataset was created for the Kaggle MedGemma Impact Challenge to test the hypothesis
that contextual signals (claim veracity, image-claim alignment) outperform pixel-level
forensics for detecting real-world medical misinformation.

## Dataset Composition

**Total samples:** 160 image-claim pairs

### Source 1: BTD MRI Test Sets (120 samples, authentic images)

- **Origin:** Back-in-Time Diffusion (BTD) MRI and CT Deepfake Test Sets
- **Kaggle source:** https://www.kaggle.com/datasets/freddiegraboski/btd-mri-and-ct-deepfake-test-sets
- **GitHub:** https://github.com/FreddieMG/BTD--Unsupervised-Detection-of-Medical-Deepfakes
- **Citation:** Graboski, F., Mirsky, Y. (2024). "Back-in-Time Diffusion: Unsupervised
  Detection of Medical Deepfakes." ACM Transactions on Intelligent Systems and Technology.
  arXiv:2407.15169
- **Original license:** AGPL-3.0
- **Images used:** Authentic (non-tampered) brain MRI slices only
- **What we added:** Synthetically generated textual claims paired with each image,
  plus programmatically assigned ground truth labels for veracity and alignment

### Source 2: UCI Deepfakes: Medical Image Tamper Detection (40 samples, tampered images)

- **Origin:** UCI Machine Learning Repository, Dataset #520
- **Repository:** https://archive.ics.uci.edu/dataset/520/deepfakes+medical+image+tamper+detection
- **DOI:** 10.24432/C5J318
- **Citation:** Mirsky, Y., Mahler, T., Shelef, I., & Elovici, Y. (2019). "CT-GAN:
  Malicious Tampering of 3D Medical Imagery using Deep Learning." USENIX Security Symposium.
- **Original license:** CC BY 4.0
- **Images used:** Tampered 3D lung CT scan slices (DICOM, converted to PNG) from
  Experiment 1 (blind tampering), where cancerous tumors were either injected or removed
- **What we added:** Synthetically generated textual claims in four categories
  (true/false x aligned/misaligned), plus programmatically assigned ground truth labels

## Ground Truth Labels

Each sample has three ground truth dimensions:

1. **Pixel integrity** (pixel_authentic: true/false)
   - True for all 120 BTD samples (authentic MRI images)
   - False for all 40 UCI samples (tampered CT scans)

2. **Claim veracity** (plausibility: high/low)
   - Whether the textual claim makes a medically accurate statement
   - Assigned programmatically based on claim template category

3. **Image-claim alignment** (alignment: aligned/misaligned)
   - Whether the claim accurately describes what the image shows
   - Assigned programmatically based on claim template category

### Label Categories

| Category                  | Count | Pixel Authentic | Veracity | Alignment |
|---------------------------|-------|-----------------|----------|-----------|
| legitimate                | 30    | Yes             | High     | Aligned   |
| misleading                | 30    | Yes             | High     | Misaligned|
| intentional_misinfo       | 30    | Yes             | Low      | Various   |
| other_authentic           | 30    | Yes             | Various  | Various   |
| tampered_true_aligned     | 10    | No              | High     | Aligned   |
| tampered_true_misaligned  | 10    | No              | High     | Misaligned|
| tampered_false_aligned    | 10    | No              | Low      | Aligned   |
| tampered_false_misaligned | 10    | No              | Low      | Misaligned|

## Important Limitations and Transparency Notes

1. **Labels are NOT expert-annotated.** All ground truth labels (veracity, alignment)
   were programmatically assigned based on claim template categories, not reviewed by
   medical professionals or domain experts. This is a significant limitation.

2. **Claims are synthetic.** The textual claims paired with images were generated from
   predefined templates, not collected from real-world misinformation sources. They may
   not reflect the linguistic patterns, subtlety, or diversity of actual medical
   misinformation encountered online.

3. **Limited modalities.** The dataset contains only brain MRI (BTD) and lung CT (UCI)
   images. Results may not generalize to other medical imaging modalities (X-ray,
   ultrasound, histopathology, etc.).

4. **Small sample size.** 160 samples provides moderate statistical power. Larger
   datasets (500+) would yield stronger confidence intervals.

5. **Cross-modality mismatch.** The 40 tampered images are lung CT DICOMs while the
   120 authentic images are brain MRI PNGs — different anatomical regions and file formats.
   This creates a perfect correlation between pixel_authentic and modality/anatomical region 
   (brain MRI vs lung CT), allowing models to classify anatomy instead of detecting tampering, 
   and therefore invalidates direct comparisons between the pixel forensics baseline and 
   contextual methods. To ensure fair tampering-detection comparisons, we recommend either 
   controlling for modality (e.g., matching modalities/formats) or excluding pixel_authentic-based 
   evaluation when modalities differ.

6. **Single evaluation context.** This dataset was designed to validate the MedContext
   system specifically. Independent validation on real-world misinformation corpora is
   needed before generalizing findings.

## Dataset Format

JSON file with the following structure per sample:

```json
{
  "image_id": "string",
  "image_path": "relative/path/to/image.png",
  "claim": "Textual claim about the medical image",
  "ground_truth": {
    "pixel_authentic": true,
    "alignment": "aligned",
    "plausibility": "high",
    "is_misinformation": false,
    "label": "legitimate"
  }
}
```

## Validation Results (MedContext System)

When evaluated with MedContext's three-method approach (n=160):

- **Pixel forensics (integrity):** 25.0% accuracy — confirms pixel methods fail on
  authentic-image misinformation
- **Claim veracity:** 61.9% binary accuracy (F1: 0.591)
- **Image-claim alignment:** 60.6% binary accuracy (F1: 0.540)
- **All three dimensions correct:** 9 of 160 samples (5.6%)
- **At least two dimensions correct:** 88 of 160 samples (55.0%)

These results support the thesis that contextual signals outperform pixel forensics
for detecting medical misinformation, while honestly reflecting the early-stage
performance of the contextual analysis components.

## Reproduction

The dataset construction scripts are available in the MedContext repository:
- `scripts/create_3d_dataset.py` — merges BTD and UCI sources
- `scripts/add_misleading_cases.py` — reclassifies label categories
- `scripts/validate_three_methods.py` — runs the three-method evaluation

Repository: https://github.com/drjforrest/MedContext-impact
